Why I started MedMinute

I created MedMinute because medical information often feels harder to understand than it needs to be. Students and families may hear unfamiliar terms, receive laboratory results or experience symptoms without knowing how the pieces connect. MedMinute aims to turn complex ideas into clear, responsible educational explanations.

The project is not intended to diagnose illness or replace healthcare professionals. Its purpose is to help people become more informed, ask better questions and feel less intimidated by medicine.

My journey

The experiences that shaped MedMinute.

01

Interest in science and medicine

Biology, chemistry and the relationship between the body's systems developed into a strong interest in medicine and endocrinology.

02

March 2025: hypothyroidism diagnosis

After experiencing fatigue, lethargy, weakness, brain fog and reduced performance in school and sports, I was diagnosed with hypothyroidism. Understanding tests such as TSH and free T4, and learning which specialists manage thyroid disease, took time.

03

Learning independently

Because the symptoms were initially difficult to connect, I spent significant time learning how the thyroid works and how hormone disorders can affect energy, mood, concentration and daily life.

04

Hospital internship

My hospital experience gave me direct exposure to healthcare teamwork, patient communication, professionalism and the importance of accurate observation.

05

Health Education Club in progress

I am working toward developing a school-based health education initiative that can make science and healthcare topics more engaging for students.

06

Creating MedMinute

MedMinute brings these interests together through medical articles, Alberta curriculum study support, career guides, clinical skills, interactive cases and public-health education.

Why education matters

Health education can reduce confusion, improve communication and help people recognize when persistent or urgent symptoms deserve professional attention. Clear information also allows students to explore healthcare careers more realistically.

Future plans

Over the coming months, I hope to publish more original articles, create educational videos, speak with healthcare professionals, develop Arabic resources and expand opportunities for students interested in medicine.
