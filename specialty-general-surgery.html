Specialty Guide

General Surgery

1 min estimatedLast updated July 15, 2026

Operative and non-operative surgical care

General surgeons diagnose and treat conditions that may require an operation, particularly involving the abdomen, digestive tract, soft tissues and emergency surgery.

Training pathway

Stage 1: Medical degree

Complete medical school and licensing.

Stage 2: General surgery residency

Train in operative technique, trauma, perioperative care, intensive care and surgical decision-making.

Stage 3: Optional fellowship

Subspecialize in colorectal, breast, endocrine, hepatobiliary, transplant, trauma or minimally invasive surgery.

Important: The exact route and training length differ between Qatar, Ireland, the United Kingdom, Canada, the United States and other systems.

What the daily work actually involves

  • Evaluate patients in clinic and emergency settings.
  • Perform operations and supervise postoperative recovery.
  • Conduct ward rounds and monitor complications.
  • Interpret imaging and decide whether surgery is necessary.
  • Coordinate with anesthesiology, radiology, pathology and medical teams.
Typical day: A surgeon may begin with early ward rounds, spend several hours operating and finish with clinic or emergency consultations.

Common conditions and responsibilities

  • Appendicitis
  • Gallbladder disease
  • Hernias
  • Bowel obstruction and perforation
  • Breast and thyroid surgical disease
  • Abdominal trauma

Who may enjoy this specialty?

This field may suit someone who enjoys scientific problem-solving, direct patient care, teamwork and continued learning.

  • Strong communication and empathy
  • Careful attention to detail
  • Ability to work calmly under pressure
  • Comfort with uncertainty and complex decisions
  • Commitment to professional ethics
