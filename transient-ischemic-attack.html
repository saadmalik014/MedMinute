Transient Ischemic Attack

1 min estimatedLast updated July 15, 2026

Why temporary stroke-like symptoms still require urgent assessment.

Educational purpose: General information only; not a diagnosis or substitute for medical care.
Approximately 2 min readLast updated July 15, 2026

What it is

A transient ischemic attack causes temporary neurological symptoms from disrupted blood flow without lasting infarction on modern definitions.

Symptoms

The symptoms can be identical to stroke: facial weakness, arm weakness, speech trouble, vision change or imbalance.

Why it matters

A TIA can be a warning that a stroke may occur soon.

Assessment

Evaluation looks for atrial fibrillation, carotid disease, blood-pressure problems, diabetes and other treatable causes.

Action

Do not wait to see if symptoms improve. Seek emergency medical assessment.

MedMinute takeaway

Persistent, severe or worsening symptoms should be discussed with a qualified healthcare professional.

References and further reading

  • CDC and MedlinePlus condition overviews
  • NIDDK endocrine, diabetes and digestive disease resources
  • NHS condition guides
  • Alberta and international emergency-care principles

Quick facts

  • Common abbreviation: TIA
  • Symptoms resolve, but risk remains
  • Emergency evaluation is needed
