Medical Case Challenges

Think through 24 cases.

Endocrinology

Case 1

A 15-year-old has excessive thirst, frequent urination, weight loss and fatigue. What is the most likely diagnosis?

The classic osmotic symptoms and weight loss strongly suggest new-onset type 1 diabetes. Urgent glucose and ketone assessment is required.
Neurology

Case 2

A person suddenly develops facial droop, arm weakness and slurred speech. What is the priority?

These are stroke warning signs. Treatment is time-sensitive.
Cardiology

Case 3

A patient has an irregularly irregular pulse and palpitations. Which rhythm is most likely?

Atrial fibrillation classically produces an irregularly irregular rhythm.
Pulmonology

Case 4

A teenager has episodic wheeze, cough and chest tightness that worsen with exercise. What test commonly supports diagnosis?

Spirometry with bronchodilator response can demonstrate variable airflow obstruction.
Gastroenterology

Case 5

Pain begins near the navel and moves to the lower right abdomen with nausea. What is most concerning?

Migration of pain to the right lower quadrant is a classic appendicitis pattern.
Vascular

Case 6

An older smoker has a pulsating abdominal mass and sudden severe back pain. What should be suspected?

This combination is an emergency and may represent AAA rupture.
Autoimmune

Case 7

A young person has joint pain, photosensitive rash, mouth ulcers and protein in urine. Which condition fits best?

Multisystem inflammatory findings can suggest lupus and require specialist evaluation.
Endocrinology

Case 8

TSH is high and free T4 is low. Which pattern does this support?

A failing thyroid leads to low T4 and compensatory high TSH.
Emergency

Case 9

A person with asthma cannot speak full sentences and their reliever inhaler is not helping. What is the best response?

Severe breathlessness with poor response to rescue treatment is an emergency.
Hematology

Case 10

A student has fatigue, pallor, low hemoglobin and low ferritin. What is most likely?

Low ferritin supports depleted iron stores.
Neurology

Case 11

A patient has the worst sudden headache of their life with neck stiffness. What diagnosis must be excluded urgently?

A thunderclap headache can indicate aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage.
Nephrology

Case 12

A patient has reduced eGFR and albumin in urine for more than three months. What does this suggest?

Persistent reduced function or kidney damage for at least three months meets CKD criteria.
Cardiology

Case 13

Chest pressure occurs during exertion and improves with rest. What does this pattern suggest?

Predictable exertional chest pressure relieved by rest can reflect myocardial ischemia.
Dermatology

Case 14

A teenager has inflammatory papules, pustules and comedones. What is the likely condition?

Comedones plus inflammatory lesions are typical of acne.
Gastroenterology

Case 15

A patient has burning behind the breastbone and sour regurgitation after meals. What is most likely?

Heartburn and regurgitation are common GERD symptoms.
Mental Health

Case 16

Low mood, loss of interest, sleep change and poor concentration have lasted several weeks and impair school. What should happen?

Persistent impairment warrants assessment; urgent support is needed if there are safety concerns.
Orthopedics

Case 17

After an ankle injury, there is deformity, severe pain and inability to bear weight. What is the appropriate next step?

Possible fracture or dislocation requires stabilization and prompt evaluation.
Infectious Disease

Case 18

Fever, rapid breathing, confusion and low blood pressure occur during an infection. What is the concern?

Organ dysfunction during infection can indicate sepsis, a medical emergency.
Endocrinology

Case 19

A person has tremor, heat intolerance, weight loss and palpitations with low TSH and high free T4. What is likely?

The symptoms and laboratory pattern support excess thyroid hormone.
Rheumatology

Case 20

Symmetric swollen hand joints with prolonged morning stiffness suggest which disease?

Persistent symmetric small-joint inflammation is characteristic of rheumatoid arthritis.
Pulmonology

Case 21

Loud snoring, witnessed breathing pauses and daytime sleepiness suggest what?

Repeated airway collapse during sleep causes these symptoms.
Gastroenterology

Case 22

Upper abdominal pain with black stools is most concerning for what?

Melena can indicate upper gastrointestinal bleeding and needs urgent evaluation.
Cardiology

Case 23

A patient with atrial fibrillation is assessed for anticoagulation mainly to prevent what complication?

Atrial fibrillation can allow clots to form and travel to the brain.
Neurology

Case 24

Recurrent unprovoked seizures are the defining feature of what condition?

Epilepsy is a lasting predisposition to recurrent unprovoked seizures.
