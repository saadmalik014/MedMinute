Heart Attack

1 min estimatedLast updated July 15, 2026

What happens when blood flow to heart muscle is suddenly blocked, common warning signs and emergency treatment.

Educational purpose: General information only; not a diagnosis or substitute for medical care.
Approximately 2 min readLast updated July 15, 2026

What happens

A heart attack occurs when a coronary artery becomes blocked long enough to injure or kill part of the heart muscle.

Possible symptoms

Pressure, heaviness or pain in the chest; shortness of breath; sweating; nausea; unusual fatigue; or discomfort in the jaw, back or arm. Symptoms can vary.

Diagnosis

Clinicians use the history, ECG changes and cardiac troponin blood testing. Imaging or coronary angiography may follow.

Treatment

Rapid treatment may include antiplatelet medicines and urgent restoration of blood flow through angioplasty and a stent. Other medicines reduce strain on the heart and prevent recurrence.

Emergency action

Do not drive yourself. In Qatar, call 999 for suspected heart attack.

MedMinute takeaway

Persistent, severe or worsening symptoms should be discussed with a qualified healthcare professional.
