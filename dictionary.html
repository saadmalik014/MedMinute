Medical Dictionary

82 essential medical terms.

4 min estimatedLast updated July 15, 2026

A searchable student reference.

TermDefinitionField
A1C (HbA1c)A blood test estimating average glucose over roughly two to three months.Endocrinology
AblationDeliberate destruction or removal of tissue, often using heat, cold, energy or chemicals.Procedures
AcidosisA state in which blood or body fluids are excessively acidic.General Medicine
AcuteSudden in onset or short in duration.General Medicine
AdenomaA usually benign tumor arising from glandular tissue.Pathology
Adrenal glandA hormone-producing gland located above each kidney.Endocrinology
AneurysmAbnormal localized widening of an artery caused by wall weakness.Vascular Medicine
AnginaChest discomfort caused by insufficient oxygen delivery to heart muscle.Cardiology
AntibodyA protein made by immune cells that recognizes a specific target.Immunology
AnticoagulantA medicine that reduces formation or growth of blood clots.Hematology
AntigenA molecule recognized by the immune system.Immunology
ArrhythmiaAn abnormal heart rhythm.Cardiology
ArteryA blood vessel carrying blood away from the heart.Cardiology
Autoimmune diseaseA condition in which immune activity targets the body's own tissues.Rheumatology
BenignNoncancerous; does not invade nearby tissue or metastasize.Oncology
BiopsyRemoval of tissue or cells for laboratory examination.Pathology
BronchioleA small airway branch inside the lungs.Pulmonology
BronchodilatorA medicine that relaxes airway smooth muscle and widens airways.Pulmonology
CapillaryA microscopic vessel where gases, nutrients and wastes are exchanged.Cardiology
CarcinomaA cancer arising from epithelial cells.Oncology
Cardiac outputVolume of blood pumped by the heart each minute.Cardiology
ChronicPersistent or long-lasting.General Medicine
CirrhosisAdvanced scarring and distortion of the liver.Gastroenterology
CT scanImaging that uses X-rays and computer processing to create cross-sectional pictures.Radiology
CyanosisBluish discoloration from inadequate oxygenation or circulation.Emergency Medicine
DiagnosisIdentification of a disease or condition based on evidence.General Medicine
DiastolePhase when the heart relaxes and fills with blood.Cardiology
Differential diagnosisA list of possible explanations for a patient's findings.General Medicine
DyspneaThe medical term for shortness of breath.Pulmonology
EdemaSwelling caused by excess fluid in tissues.General Medicine
EmbolusMaterial, often a clot, traveling through blood and blocking a vessel.Vascular Medicine
Endocrine glandA gland that releases hormones into the bloodstream.Endocrinology
EndoscopyUse of a camera-equipped instrument to view internal structures.Gastroenterology
EnzymeA biological catalyst that speeds chemical reactions.Biochemistry
EtiologyThe cause or origin of a disease.General Medicine
FibrosisFormation of excess scar-like connective tissue.Pathology
GlomerulusA kidney capillary network that filters blood.Nephrology
GlucoseA simple sugar used as a major cellular fuel.Endocrinology
HemoglobinOxygen-carrying protein inside red blood cells.Hematology
HemorrhageBleeding from a damaged blood vessel.Emergency Medicine
HomeostasisMaintenance of stable internal conditions.Physiology
HormoneA chemical messenger transported to target cells.Endocrinology
HypertensionPersistently elevated arterial blood pressure.Cardiology
HypotensionAbnormally low blood pressure.Cardiology
HypoxiaInsufficient oxygen at the tissue level.Pulmonology
IatrogenicCaused unintentionally by medical care or treatment.General Medicine
ImmunityThe body's ability to recognize and respond to threats.Immunology
InflammationA protective biological response to injury or harmful stimuli.Immunology
InsulinA pancreatic hormone that helps cells take up and store glucose.Endocrinology
IschemiaInadequate blood flow and oxygen delivery to tissue.Cardiology
LesionAn area of abnormal or damaged tissue.Pathology
LeukocyteA white blood cell involved in immune defense.Hematology
MalignantCancerous, with the ability to invade or spread.Oncology
MetabolismAll chemical reactions that sustain life.Biochemistry
MetastasisSpread of cancer from its original site to another location.Oncology
MRIImaging using magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed pictures.Radiology
MyocardiumThe muscular layer of the heart responsible for contraction.Cardiology
NephronThe microscopic functional unit of the kidney.Nephrology
NeuronA cell specialized for electrical and chemical communication.Neurology
NeuropathyDisease or damage affecting nerves.Neurology
OncologyThe field focused on cancer.Oncology
PathogenA microorganism capable of causing disease.Infectious Diseases
PerfusionDelivery of blood to tissue.Cardiology
PlateletA blood component essential for clot formation.Hematology
PrognosisExpected course or outcome of a condition.General Medicine
ProphylaxisAction taken to prevent disease or complications.Preventive Medicine
PulmonaryRelating to the lungs.Pulmonology
ReceptorA molecule that binds a signal and triggers a cellular response.Biochemistry
RemissionReduction or disappearance of disease signs and symptoms.Oncology
SepsisLife-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated response to infection.Emergency Medicine
SpirometryA breathing test measuring airflow and lung volumes.Pulmonology
StenosisAbnormal narrowing of a passage or opening.General Medicine
SyncopeTemporary loss of consciousness from reduced brain perfusion.Cardiology
SystolePhase when the heart contracts and ejects blood.Cardiology
TachycardiaA faster-than-expected heart rate.Cardiology
ThrombusA blood clot that forms within a vessel or the heart.Hematology
TriagePrioritization of patients according to urgency.Emergency Medicine
TumorAn abnormal mass of tissue; it may be benign or malignant.Oncology
UltrasoundImaging that uses high-frequency sound waves.Radiology
VaccineA preparation that trains the immune system to recognize a pathogen or antigen.Immunology
VeinA vessel carrying blood toward the heart.Cardiology
VentilationMovement of air into and out of the lungs.Pulmonology
